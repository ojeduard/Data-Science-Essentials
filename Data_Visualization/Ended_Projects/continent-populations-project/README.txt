
Data Source:
Gapminder.org

Population:
Total population counts the number of inhabitants in the region.

Life expectancy:
The number of years a newborn infant would live if the current mortality rates at different ages were to stay the same throughout its life.

Child mortality:
This is the probability of dying between birth and exactly five years of age expressed per 1,000 live births.

Babies per woman:
This is the number of children that  would be born to a woman if she were to live to the end of her childbearing years and bear children in accordance  with age-specific fertility rates of the specified year. It expresses the statistical indicator total fertility rate.

Income per person:
This is real GDP per capita (gross domestic product per person adjusted for inflation) converted to international dollars using purchasing power parity rates. An international dollar has the same purchasing power over GDP as the U.S. dollar has in the United States. GDP at purchaser's prices is the sum of gross value added by all resident producers in the economy plus any product taxes and minus any subsidies not included in the value of the products. It is calculated without making deductions for depreciation of fabricated assets or for depletion and degradation of natural resources. Data are in constant 2021 international dollars.
